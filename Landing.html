3.2 Scaled dot-product attention

The core operation of the Transformer computes attention over a set of values, weighted by the compatibility of queries and keys:

Attention(Q, K, V) = softmax(QKT√dk) V

where Q, K and V are matrices of queries, keys and values.

Click to explain & read aloud
ƒx Explain this equation
softmax(QKT√dk) V
This is scaled dot-product attention — the heart of a Transformer.
QKᵀ scores how well each query matches every key.
Dividing by √dₖ keeps those scores from growing too large.
softmax turns the scores into weights that sum to one.
Multiplying by V returns a weighted blend of the values.

How it works

From draft to polished in three moves

Write or paste your LaTeX

Start a project in the browser, upload your .tex and .bib, and see it compile live. No local toolchain to install.

Listen & proofread

Press play. Publify reads each sentence and highlights it, while diagnostics flag anything that won't compile or reads wrong.

Collaborate & track

Invite co-authors, resolve comments and to-dos, and rely on version history so every change is attributed and recoverable.
